Livingston

Livingston Skate Park was the first of its kind when opened in 1981.Livingston is a internationally renowned park and was inspired by skateparks in the United States, and has attracted skaters from all over the world, including skating legend Tony Hawk.

Livingston Skate Park

Stoke Plaza

Stoke Plaza is a huge all concrete course that was built in 2010. Stoke Plaza is an amazing facility and if you are a street skater this is a must visit. There are endless numbers of lines that can be hit here and a lot of space between obstacles to adjust between tricks.

Skate Plaza

The Level

Opened in June 2013 it is now the centre of the skate scene in Brighton and for good reason. The street section holds everything that a street skater could possibly need and features banks, hips, stair sets, rails, kickers, flat and curved ledges, a pyramid, a pole jam and some pretty unique contours that you will struggle to find anywhere else.

The Level

Source Park

The Source Park opened in March 2016 to become what has been called the world's largest underground skate park. Source Park is a huge underground skatepark located in the heart of Hastings. With state of the art facilities including, mosaic pillars, brass handrails and the original steam pump engine.
